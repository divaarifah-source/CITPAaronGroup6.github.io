Detailed Design

Integrated Regenerative Coastal System (IRCS)

Combining natural processes and low-tech design for sustainable coastal protection on Saibai Island.

How the IRCS Operates

The IRCS is a hybrid living shoreline system built on simple engineering paired with ecological regeneration. It slows tidal energy, captures suspended sediment, and guides mangrove establishment until a living coastal barrier rebuilds itself.

  • Porous brushwood structures calm water without blocking tidal exchange.
  • Captured sediment raises the tidal flat, matching mangrove rooting elevations.
  • Community-led mangrove planting accelerates natural succession.

Validation Against Design Criteria

Ecological Harmony

Restores tidal wetland processes, enables native mangrove regrowth, and enhances biodiversity through habitat complexity.

Community Empowerment

Uses local materials, builds skills through the Community Steering Group, and aligns with cultural stewardship.

Physical Protection

Dissipates wave energy by up to 70%, raises land elevation, and forms a living breakwater that strengthens annually.

Implementation Feasibility

Saibai Island can implement IRCS using locally available materials and labour, supported by carbon revenue and reduced maintenance costs.

  • Materials: Brushwood, bamboo, coconut fibre, and mangrove propagules sourced on island.
  • People: Community Steering Group coordinates paid local crews and training.
  • Financing: Blue Carbon credits and avoided seawall repairs reduce net cost.

Limitations & Future Testing

The prototype validates sediment trapping, yet full-scale pilots on Saibai will refine brushwood density, bund spacing, and sediment sourcing for peak performance. Continuous monitoring is required to adapt to sea-level rise, cyclones, and ecological feedback.
