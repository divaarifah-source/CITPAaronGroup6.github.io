Project Rollout Overview

Building the IRCS starts with local prep, gathering materials, and training the community before spreading along Saibai's at-risk shoreline.

Our plan keeps decisions on Country: we design test sections with the Community Steering Group (CSG), check the system using local knowledge, and only grow bigger when the community is ready to lead.

Community Engagement Strategy

Traditional knowledge and design-together sessions guide each choice so the IRCS fits cultural ways.

The IRCS is designed with Saibai elders. Seasonal calendars guide when to harvest, and workshops make sure every youth, ranger, and elder can understand the shore's changes.

Storytelling & Mapping Workshops

Share tide stories, old erosion spots, and cultural places to put fences where community memory says.

Skill-Building Sessions

Hands-on stick fence building, tying, and mud testing so locals can grow the system themselves.

Local Stewardship Training

Risk & Mitigation

Backup plans keep the test flexible when weather, materials, or people change.

Risk Impact Mitigation Strategy
Cyclone or High Storm Season Fence damage or loss. Build fence pieces that can be swapped fast; build outside cyclone season.
Material Shortage Delayed building. Harvest in stages and get sticks from multiple nearby islands.
Labour or Engagement Drop Slower progress and missing know-how. Keep paying people via Blue Carbon money and rotate jobs.
Poor Sediment Retention Less shore recovery. Change fence spacing and holes, add more mud based on checking results.

Long-Term Vision

The test builds the base for a Saibai-led "Blue Belt" across the Torres Strait.

The IRCS proves that community-built, nature-based fixes can heal coastlines. After test success, we spread the model to other islands, mixing stick fences with traditional care and blue carbon money.

Future State

Connected healing systems restore nature, protect jobs, and pay for community care for decades.

Why This is Financial Sovereignty

Unlike grant-based projects, the IRCS creates ongoing income managed on Country. After an 8‚Äì10 year payback period, 100% of Blue Carbon money stays on Saibai to pay for education, health, cultural programs, and future climate fixes. The community shifts from climate risk to climate business.
