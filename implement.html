Project Rollout Overview

Building the IRCS starts with local prep, gathering materials, and training the community before spreading along Saibai's at-risk shoreline.

Our plan keeps decisions on Country: we design test sections with the Community Steering Group (CSG), check the system using local knowledge, and only grow bigger when the community is ready to lead.

24-Month Implementation Roadmap

A two-year program moves from planning and building through planting and checking, with Saibai leaders at every step.

Phase 1: Foundation

Months 1–6

Planning

Community Engagement

  • Co-design workshops with Saibai elders and youth.
  • Traditional knowledge integration sessions.
  • Local employment training program launch.

Technical Preparation

  • Environmental impact assessment and permits.
  • Detailed site surveys and sediment analysis.
  • Material procurement (local timber sourcing).
  • Blue Carbon baseline assessment.

Phase 2: Installation

Months 7–12

Construction

Bund Deployment

  • Install 2 km of brushwood bunds (pilot section).
  • 50% local labour (15–20 community members employed).
  • Monthly monitoring of sediment accumulation.

Sediment Monitoring

  • Install sensor array (water level, sediment load).
  • Bi-weekly topographic surveys.
  • Partnership with James Cook University for research.
  • Community-led observation protocol training.

Phase 3: Restoration

Months 13–18

Planting

Mangrove Planting

  • Propagule collection from healthy donor sites.
  • 10,000+ seedlings planted in accreted zones.
  • Community planting days (cultural significance).
  • Initial survival monitoring (target: 70%+).

Ecosystem Development

  • Natural colonization by native species.
  • Fish and crab habitat surveys.
  • Bird nesting monitoring.
  • Community education on ecosystem services.

Phase 4: Validation

Months 19–24

Verification

Performance Assessment

  • Final sediment accretion measurement (target: 20 cm+).
  • Mangrove growth and survival evaluation.
  • Storm event resilience testing (natural cyclones).
  • Comparison with unprotected control sections.

Blue Carbon Certification

  • Verra VCS + CCB Standards verification audit.
  • Carbon stock quantification (soil + biomass).
  • First carbon credit issuance (estimated 500–800 credits).
  • Revenue distribution plan finalized with community.

Roles & Responsibilities

Every partner has a clear job so the IRCS stays on track and community-driven.

Stakeholder Role in Implementation
Community Steering Group (CSG) Runs local work, checks sites, and approves how materials are used.
TSIRC (Torres Strait Island Regional Council) Helps with logistics, permits, and government links.
University Team Checks design works, shares test data, and writes reports every 3 months.
EWB Support Watches over program, mentors engineers, and connects to wider experts.
Local Labour Force Leads building, planting, regular upkeep, and data collection.

Resource & Budget Snapshot

A small test budget focuses on skill-building while getting ready for future growth.

While the IRCS leans on low-cost, natural materials, real delivery must cover transport, permits, labour, and community engagement so work on Country stays culturally led and safe.

The six-month pilot stays compact yet credible, with an indicative envelope of AUD 30k–50k. More than half is earmarked for Saibai labour so Indigenous leadership and employment remain at the core.

Blue Carbon Revenue Pathway

Documented carbon gains turn the IRCS into a self-funded coastal care program owned by Saibai community.

By restoring mangroves, our project can earn ‚Äúblue carbon‚Äù credits ‚Äî a way to get paid for helping nature capture carbon from the air. Each year, even a small area of healthy mangroves can bring in hundreds of dollars for the community. As the mangroves grow stronger and the land builds up, this income can increase, helping support local jobs and future care for Saibai‚Äôs coastline.

Credit Generation

Baseline and Year-2 surveys support Verra VCS + CCB pathways. A mature two-year pilot could issue 500–800 credits, depending on final footprint.

Revenue Allocation

Early credit sales feed a coastal stewardship fund that covers wages, maintenance materials, and rollout of new IRCS cells.

Enterprise Build

As volumes grow, Saibai can mentor neighbouring islands, offering fee-for-service training in mangrove care and carbon monitoring.

Risk & Mitigation

Backup plans keep the test flexible when weather, materials, or people change.

Risk Impact Mitigation Strategy
Cyclone or High Storm Season Fence damage or loss. Build fence pieces that can be swapped fast; build outside cyclone season.
Material Shortage Delayed building. Harvest in stages and get sticks from multiple nearby islands.
Labour or Engagement Drop Slower progress and missing know-how. Keep paying people via Blue Carbon money and rotate jobs.
Poor Sediment Retention Less shore recovery. Change fence spacing and holes, add more mud based on checking results.

Why This is Financial Sovereignty

Unlike grant-based projects, the IRCS creates ongoing income managed on Country. After an 8‚Äì10 year payback period, 100% of Blue Carbon money stays on Saibai to pay for education, health, cultural programs, and future climate fixes. The community shifts from climate risk to climate business.
