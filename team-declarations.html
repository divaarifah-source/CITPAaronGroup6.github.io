Guiding Pillars

Declarations in practice

Four commitments ensure every IRCS activity protects culture, people, and place.

Cultural Safety

  • Sacred sites, story places, and seasonal boundaries mapped with Saibai knowledge holders.
  • Protocols for photography, data sharing, and ceremony attendance recorded in the field manual.
  • Community steering group empowered with veto rights on design changes.

Technical Integrity

  • Design assumptions logged with version control and peer review sign-off.
  • All calculations trace back to referenced datasets with uncertainty bounds noted.
  • Independent safety factors applied to bund spacing, materials, and tidal modelling.

Transparent Governance

  • Decisions documented in open meeting notes shared with TSRC and the community.
  • Conflict-of-interest declarations refreshed ahead of each procurement milestone.
  • Budget and resourcing dashboards published alongside project status updates.

Environmental Stewardship

  • Blue carbon estimates validated against conservative regional benchmarks.
  • Mangrove rehabilitation plans co-developed with Rangers for long-term custodianship.
  • Adaptive monitoring triggers activated when erosion, salinity, or biodiversity trends shift.

Assurance pathway

Milestones are verified collaboratively with Torres Strait Regional Council, UTS supervisors, and the Saibai Steering Group. Each review stage captures evidence, voices, and lessons learned before the next phase proceeds.

Evidence packs live in the shared IRCS repository with restricted access for community custodians and project partners.

  1. Project charter confirmed

    April 2025 — Charter co-signed by TSRC leadership, community Elders, and UTS FEIT faculty.

  2. Design validation workshop

    June 2025 — Bund configuration and materials validated against coastal modelling and cultural overlays.

  3. Implementation readiness review

    August 2025 — Workforce plans, procurement pathways, and risk registers endorsed before mobilisation.

  4. Ongoing assurance cycle

    Quarterly from September 2025 — Field data, cultural reflections, and financial performance reviewed with the community.

Declaration sign-off

The declarations were formally endorsed on 20 September 2025 following a joint workshop with the Saibai Community Steering Group. Any scope adjustments trigger a rapid review and updated sign-off within five working days.
