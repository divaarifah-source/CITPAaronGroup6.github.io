Integrated Restorative Coastal System (IRCS)

We're helping a small island community fight rising seas—using nature instead of concrete walls.

Our solution works with the land, not against it. By restoring coastal forests and rebuilding natural defenses, we're protecting homes, preserving culture, and creating jobs—all while fighting climate change.

What is IRCS?

"

The Integrated Regenerative Coastal System (IRCS) is a low-tech, community-led solution designed to protect Saibai Island from coastal erosion. It restores the island's tidal wetlands by combining brushwood bunds, sediment nourishment, and mangrove regeneration into one living shoreline system. The project empowers the local community to manage their environment while strengthening both ecological and cultural resilience.

"

Project Overview

The IRCS is a pioneering initiative designed for the Torres Strait Regional Council and Saibai Island Community. It blends ecological restoration with social and economic benefits, aiming for sustainability through the Triple Bottom Line Framework. As we face climate challenges, IRCS advocates resilience and regeneration for future generations.

Our Approach

Community Impact

The IRCS empowers the Saibai community by combining traditional knowledge with low-tech, nature-based coastal protection. It restores local ecosystems while strengthening cultural resilience and ownership.

Financial Sustainability

By replacing costly seawalls with regenerative systems, the IRCS reduces maintenance costs and introduces revenue potential through Blue Carbon credits and fisheries enhancement.

Long-Term Viability

The system is self-sustaining — once mangroves are established, they continuously trap sediment, rebuild land, and maintain the shoreline without external input.
