Executive Snapshot

Integrated Restorative Coastal System (IRCS)

Community-led coastal repair slowing Saibai’s 0.6 m a year shoreline loss.

IRCS ties 300 m of brushwood cells, 4,500 mangrove seedlings, and Saibai crews to capture 18 tonnes of mud each spring tide, softening the 3.59 m king tides that flooded homes 28 times last wet season.

Acknowledgement of Country

We acknowledge the Traditional Owners of Saibai Island, the Kaurareg and Saibai Islander peoples of the Torres Strait, and pay our respects to Elders past and present. We recognise the enduring custodianship of land, sea, and sky that guides every decision for the Integrated Regenerative Coastal System.

Our UTS Faculty of Engineering and IT student team commits to listening first, embedding culture-led design, and ensuring knowledge shared with us remains on Country. We honour the contributions of the Community Steering Group, whose leadership anchors this work.

We also extend this respect to all Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples and to the communities who continue to care for Country across Australia.

What is the IRCS?

"

The Integrated Regenerative Coastal System is Saibai’s way of answering higher tides with culture and science together. Brushwood fences slow the waves, sediment gardens lift the flats 20–40 centimetres, and community nurseries replant the mangroves that once shielded the village. Elders guide every decision so knowledge stays on Country.

"

Project Overview

Co-designed with Torres Strait Regional Council, Saibai Rangers, and Elders, Stage 1 installs eight modular sediment cells across 300 m of foreshore. The pilot creates 24 local jobs, cuts annual maintenance spend by 42% compared with the 2019 seawall patch, and builds a pathway to blue-carbon income.

Our Approach

Community Impact

Twenty-four Saibai crew roles, 60 on-island training days, and Elders co-chairing toolbox yarns keep control with the community.

Financial Sustainability

Brushwood bundles avoid the $18k per barge freight bill of concrete repairs, cut annual upkeep by 42%, and unlock $55 per tonne through blue-carbon reporting.

Long-Term Viability

Sediment gardens raise tidal flats 20–40 cm so mangroves close the gaps and hold 75% of the captured mud without outside crews.
