Protecting Saibai Island's Future

We're helping a small island community fight rising seas—using nature instead of concrete walls.

Our solution works with the land, not against it. By restoring coastal forests and rebuilding natural defenses, we're protecting homes, preserving culture, and creating jobs—all while fighting climate change.

How It Works

"

Instead of expensive concrete seawalls that crack and fail, we're building with nature. Think of it as helping the island heal itself: wooden structures slow the waves, trapped soil builds up the land, and coastal forests grow back to create a living shield. The local community leads the work, uses traditional knowledge, and maintains it themselves—no costly repairs, no outside experts needed.

"

Project Overview

Saibai Island is sinking. Rising seas flood homes and threaten to wash away an entire community's history. But there's hope. We're working with the Torres Strait Regional Council and Saibai Islanders on a groundbreaking solution that protects their island, creates jobs, and fights climate change—all at once. No expensive walls. No outside maintenance crews. Just smart, sustainable design that puts the community in control.

Our Approach

Put the Community First

This isn't something done to the community—it's done by them. Local knowledge guides every decision. Community members do the work and maintain the system. Their culture and connection to the land stays strong.

Save Money Long-Term

Traditional concrete seawalls cost millions and keep breaking. Our natural approach costs less to build and practically maintains itself. Plus, the restored forests can earn money through carbon credits and fishing—turning protection into profit.

Built to Last

Once the coastal forests take root, they do the work for us. Trees trap soil, build up the shoreline, and block waves—naturally and continuously. No repairs. No expensive maintenance. Just a thriving ecosystem protecting the island for generations.
