Design Journey

Full IRCS Design Phases

A four-step evolution from restoring flow to sustaining a living coastline.

Each stage unlocks the next: once tides breathe again, bunds can settle, mangroves take root, and the community keeps the loop alive.

Phase-by-phase view

Four connected moves bring IRCS from concept to a living coastal system.

Phase 1

Foundational

Weeks 0–12

Restore flow

Core moves

  • Survey, reopen, and clean blocked tidal lanes.
  • Guide water with gentle berms to avoid inland pooling.
  • Walk Country with Elders to respect cultural boundaries.

Phase 2

Structural

Weeks 13–22

Calm the tides

Core moves

  • Build brushwood bunds with local stakes and fibre rope.
  • Lay rows parallel to shore, lightly staggered for even drag.
  • Track wave energy and sediment depth after each spring tide.

Phase 3

Ecological

Weeks 23–36

Grow the shield

Core moves

  • Plant Rhizophora stylosa propagules once mud reaches target height.
  • Pair seedlings with brush mats to guard early roots.
  • Log survival, fish habitat, and crab burrows every moon.

Phase 4

Sustainability

Weeks 37–ongoing

Keep it thriving

Core moves

  • Community crews maintain bunds, replant gaps, and log data.
  • Blue Carbon credits feed a maintenance and training fund.
  • Insights loop back into TSIRC coastal plans for expansion.

Interdependent by design: healthy tides set the stage for bunds, bunds ready the mud for mangroves, and sustained stewardship keeps every layer working together.

From design to legacy

The IRCS phases form a regenerative cycle, not a one-off build. With Saibai guiding each handover, the coastline strengthens, earns, and heals for generations.
