Technical Annex: Engineering the Integrated Regenerative Coastal System (IRCS)

Full details of design methods, materials, and tests backing the IRCS solution.

Water Flow Nature Community Building

System Overview Diagram

The IRCS mixes tide fixing, built fences, and nature healing into one flexible shoreline system.

Component Breakdown

Details by step show how water flow, materials, and care line up to heal Saibai's shoreline.

3.1 Foundational Preparation

Water Flow Land Shape

Techniques

  • Tidal Channel Reopening: Clear blocked creeks to bring back tide flow, nutrients, and salt flushing.
  • Sediment Nourishment: Place local sand and mud to rebuild tidal flats and lift heights.

Key Engineering Parameters

  • Channel depth: 0.5–1.2 m depending on site slope.
  • Mud particle size: 0.2–1.0 mm (fine sand for sticky healing).
  • Preferred source: Reused dug-up dirt from tidal channel clearing.

3.2 Structural Protection

Fence Building Water Movement

Technique & Materials

  • Stick fences built from bamboo poles, mangrove sticks, and coconut or natural rope ties.
  • Fence spacing: 2–3 m; height set at mid-tide level.
  • Target holes: 40–60% to spread wave power while letting tide through.

Engineering Function

Fences slow wave power, make a calm mud zone, and give mangroves a frame to grow on.

"In our university test, a simple friction wall kept about 75% of mud after fake wave hits—proving the same water flow idea."

3.3 Ecological Restoration

Nature

Techniques

  • Seed Spreading: Hand-plant Rhizophora stylosa and Avicennia marina behind fences.
  • Floating Plant Mats: Hold seedlings steady on deeper mudflats until roots take hold.

Performance Indicator

Mangrove survival rate 80% or better within six months when sheltered by fences and checked by the Community Steering Group.

3.4 Long-Term Sustainability

Care Checking

Method

Community-led upkeep cycles keep IRCS strong. Checks every 3 months record fence condition, mud height, and plant health.

Tools

  • Measuring sticks and mud depth markers for lines.
  • Repair logs and phone photo records shared with TSIRC.
  • Yearly reports written by the Community Steering Group.
Care Cycle

Check every 3 months → Fix or replant → Update data sheets → Share findings with CSG.

Prototype Documentation

Lab testing proves mud trapping as the IRCS's key working part.

Purpose

Show mud trapping power using clear, cheap materials to see the fence effect.

Materials

  • Plastic bottle (cut in half) with gravel, sand, soil, and grass layers.
  • 500 mL muddy water, measuring cup, and timer.
  • Control setup without plants for comparison.

Procedure

  1. Pour muddy water at steady speed through each model.
  2. Measure how clear water comes out and mud left in layers.
  3. Write down time to settle and compare planted vs control results.

Observation

Planted model kept about 75% of mud. Thick root zones increased friction, matching wetland filtering and guiding fence hole targets.
