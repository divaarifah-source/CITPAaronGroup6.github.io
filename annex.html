Technical Annex: Engineering the Integrated Regenerative Coastal System (IRCS)

Detailed documentation of design methods, materials, and validation experiments supporting the IRCS solution.

System Overview Diagram

The IRCS combines tidal restoration, engineered bunds, and ecological regeneration into a single adaptive shoreline system.

Component Breakdown

Technical details by phase show how hydrology, materials, and stewardship align to regenerate Saibai’s shoreline.

3.1 Foundational Preparation

Hydrology Geomorphology

Techniques

  • Tidal Channel Reopening: Clear blocked creeks to reinstate tidal exchange, nutrient flow, and saline flushing.
  • Sediment Nourishment: Place locally sourced sand and mud to rebuild tidal flats and raise elevations.

Key Engineering Parameters

  • Channel depth: 0.5–1.2 m depending on site gradient.
  • Sediment particle size: 0.2–1.0 mm (fine sand for cohesive healing).
  • Preferred source: Reused dredge spoil from tidal channel clearing.

3.2 Structural Protection

Bund Engineering Hydrodynamics

Technique & Materials

  • Brushwood bunds assembled from bamboo poles, mangrove brushwood, and coir or natural twine lashings.
  • Bund spacing: 2–3 m; height aligned with mid-tide level.
  • Target porosity: 40–60% to diffuse wave energy while allowing tidal exchange.

Engineering Function

Bunds attenuate wave energy, create a calm sedimentation zone, and provide a scaffold for mangrove establishment.

"In our university prototype, a simple friction barrier retained ~75% of sediment after simulated wave exposure — demonstrating the same hydrodynamic principle."

3.3 Ecological Restoration

Ecology

Techniques

  • Propagule Broadcasting: Hand-plant Rhizophora stylosa and Avicennia marina behind bunds.
  • Floating Vegetation Mats: Stabilise seedlings on deeper mudflats until root anchoring occurs.

Performance Indicator

Mangrove survival rate ≥ 80% within six months when sheltered by bunds and monitored by the Community Steering Group.

3.4 Long-Term Sustainability

Stewardship Monitoring

Method

Community-led maintenance cycles ensure IRCS resilience. Quarterly checks record bund integrity, sediment height, and vegetation condition.

Tools

  • Measuring sticks and sediment depth markers for transects.
  • Repair logs and mobile photo records shared with TSIRC.
  • Annual reports compiled by the Community Steering Group.
Stewardship Cycle

Quarterly inspection → Repair or replant → Update data sheets → Present findings to CSG.

Prototype Documentation

Laboratory testing validates sediment trapping as the IRCS’s critical performance mechanism.

Purpose

Demonstrate sediment trapping efficiency using transparent, low-cost materials to visualise the bund effect.

Materials

  • Plastic bottle (halved) with gravel, sand, soil, and grass layers.
  • 500 mL muddy water, measuring cup, and timer.
  • Control setup without vegetation for comparison.

Procedure

  1. Pour muddy water at a steady rate through each model.
  2. Measure discharge clarity and sediment left in layers.
  3. Record time to settle and compare vegetated vs control results.

Observation

Vegetated model retained approximately 75% of sediment. Dense root zones increased friction, mirroring wetland filtration and informing bund porosity targets.
